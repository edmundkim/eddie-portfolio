# Search Vision / BSG Refresh / Concept24 Dossier

## 1. Executive Summary

* The strongest current interpretation is that **Search Vision / Guided Search Vision** was the upstream, cross-platform discovery and vision effort for buyer search and lower-funnel experience, rooted in late 2023 research showing persistent problems around **guidance, selection, and trust**, with trust as the biggest issue.  
* **BSG Refresh** appears to have been a related 2024 UX research and stakeholder-alignment stream that reused the same GST framing, turned it into page-by-page buyer-experience evaluation, and pushed toward roadmap decisions. It looks adjacent to Search Vision, not identical to it.  
* **Concept24** appears to be the later concept package and visual north star that consolidated the long-range plan into an evolving prototype, linked the work to roadmap-relevant topics, and made the vision concrete enough to guide teams.   
* **Search Funnel approach / Search Funnel roadmap** looks like the operationalisation layer that translated the upstream vision into concrete big rocks such as new baseline search, filter discoverability/usability, enhanced taxonomy, easy entry/re-entry, decluttered list cards, and detail-page confidence ideas.  
* Eddie is **strongly evidenced as a core contributor with named ownership on multiple pieces**, including Vision Project UI, BSG Refresh UXR, BSG Refresh workshop planning, Concept24, new baseline search mask, and taxonomy-related work. What is **not** fully evidenced is sole leadership of the entire body of work.     
* The clearest outputs were not just workshop activity. They include a foundational research synthesis, a large Miro discovery board, benchmarking summaries, GST/opportunity framing, opportunity and solution trees, an evolving concept prototype, a north-star deck, and execution-shaping roadmaps.    
* The strongest downstream influence is on **New Search** and **Declutter / List Page simplification**. The evidence for **Detail Page / Trust** influence is also real, though a bit less direct.    
* The best current portfolio interpretation is that this work is **too substantive to exclude**, but **too overlapping and internally named to be an easy standalone case study without reframing**. It is strongest as upstream strategy context inside **New Search**, with a shorter contextual mention inside **Declutter**.

## 4. The Real Problem This Work Was Addressing

### Strongly evidenced problems

* Buyers were struggling with **guidance, selection, and trust**, and this was not a minor edge case. The late 2023 research explicitly says these were “continuing relevant user challenges”, that **trust was the biggest issue**, and that guidance-seekers had the lowest trust. 
* Trust problems were concrete, not abstract. The biggest issues were **sellers not openly sharing flaws**, **untrustworthy pictures**, incomplete information, and difficulty finding answers to open questions. This means the problem was partly listing transparency and partly confidence in the marketplace itself. 
* Search was hard to use well. Users struggled to understand filters, discover filters, narrow results, and move from too many or too few results into a confident shortlist. Search and shortlisting were time-consuming and overwhelming. 
* The product was not giving enough support for decision-making. The BSG Refresh UXR plan explicitly evaluates whether each page helps with **Guidance, Selection, Trust**, whether users understand what they can do, and whether the experience behaves like a “car buying mentor”. 
* This was also an organisational and strategic problem. The decks explicitly argue for a **shift from small incremental changes to macro-level big swings**, a **north star**, and **cross-team alignment to prioritise the largest-impact features**. That is strong evidence that the issue was not just a UX bug backlog.  
* The work was intended to connect discovery with delivery. The Miro board, Concept24 working file, and Search Funnel decks all move from research and opportunities into solution trees, prototypes, and roadmap structures. That suggests the pre-existing problem was also a lack of a coherent path from insight to aligned execution.   

### Reasonable inferences

* The underlying problem was **broader than search mechanics**. Search mattered, but the real issue was that the buyer journey from search through shortlist and decision did not feel coherent, confidence-building, or strategically directed.
* There was likely a repeated tension between **fixing basics** and **imagining a differentiated future experience**. Later strategy decks make this tension explicit, which suggests it had been unresolved during the upstream initiative too. 
* Stakeholder misalignment was probably real, but the evidence supports phrasing it as **alignment need**, not dysfunction. The workshop and roadmap artefacts show a need to converge around problems, opportunities, and sequencing.
* “Fragmentation” is partly evidenced and partly inferred. It is clearly evidenced in some later strategy material and in recurring concerns about inconsistency, but the strongest buyer-side evidence here is less about product fragmentation in the technical sense and more about **cognitive fragmentation**, uneven trust, unclear guidance, and disconnected decision support.

### Likely but not fully proven

* Eddie likely contributed to **problem framing**, because the artefacts he is attached to are the exact ones where the problem is reframed through GST and translated into research and roadmap questions.
* Eddie likely contributed to **benchmarking and opportunity mapping**, because the Miro board shows those as central activities in the discovery phase, and his later named responsibilities sit inside the same initiative chain. * There is not enough evidence to say he personally **facilitated every workshop** or authored the full Miro board.
* There is not enough evidence to say he personally owned all **analytics**, **product metrics**, or every downstream initiative.

The Concept24 working file already names baseline search and taxonomy as active work areas, with Eddie explicitly attached to both.   * Concept24 roadmap and roles on baseline search and taxonomy. 
  * 2025 Search Funnel strategy listing New baseline search, Enhanced taxonomy, Filter discoverability & usability, Easy entry/re-entry as the key search-area initiatives.

  * **Direct evidence:** The same themes and initiative names recur across Search Vision, Concept24, and 2025 Search Funnel strategy.
  * **Interpretation:** “New Search” as a portfolio label likely bundles several of these strands; the source set supports influence into that body of work, but not necessarily one neat one-to-one programme line.

* **Connection:** Very strong. The Builders all-hands deck places **Declutter list page** directly inside the Search Funnel execution roadmap beneath the Concept24 north star. ## 8. Scope And Boundaries

* **Which surfaces it touched:** It clearly touched **search mask**, **list page**, and **detail page** from the earliest Search Vision artefacts. By the Concept24 phase it also touched **homepage** and **microinteractions**, and later strategy documents extended this into recommendation and adjacent buyer-journey opportunities.   
* **Whether it was cross-platform:** Yes. The Miro board and Concept24 working materials explicitly frame the work across **iOS, Android, and web / mobile web**.  
* **Whether it was only search or broader buyer journey:** It started around lower-funnel buyer search and decision support, but it was broader than pure search mechanics. Trust, shortlist confidence, result comparison, detail-page reassurance, and later homepage/engagement concepts were inside the frame.   
* **What remained conceptual:** The Miro opportunity trees, benchmark synthesis, first-prototype steps, evolving Concept24 prototype, and much of the north-star framing were conceptual and alignment-oriented rather than shipped product.  
* **What became actionable:** Concrete roadmap items such as **new baseline search**, **filter discoverability & usability**, **enhanced taxonomy**, **easy entry / re-entry to search**, **decluttered listing cards**, and some **detail-page confidence** components became explicit big rocks or committed/exploratory initiatives.  
* **What did not clearly belong inside it:** The source set does not support treating the initiative as a full purchase/ownership programme or as a neatly bounded single product launch. It is better understood as a strategic body of upstream work that fed multiple delivery streams. 
* **What is still ambiguous:**

  * whether Search Vision and BSG Refresh were one programme with different labels or two tightly connected streams
  * exactly how Concept24 was governed relative to Search Vision
  * the precise division of ownership among Eddie and other designers/product partners
  * how much of the later 2025/2026 roadmaps should be treated as direct descendants versus strategic evolution
