# Facts for Case Study

## 1. Snapshot

- New Search was a multi-year reset of search and filters across app and web at AutoScout24.
- It mattered because search was a high-leverage path to list-page engagement and dealer leads, while the experience had become fragmented across surfaces and harder to evolve.
- The strongest evidence shows a clear iOS benchmark win and rollout, a narrower Android impact signal with weaker shipped-state proof, and a phased web launch with positive desktop signals.

## 2. Problem

- Search and filters were not operating as one coherent system. App and web had separate patterns, different friction points, and inconsistent levels of clarity.
- The issue was not just interface polish. It affected how well buyers could narrow results, understand filter state, and reach relevant inventory from the list page.
- It also mattered commercially. Search was treated as formal growth work tied to dealer lead improvement, and the existing baseline created both product friction and structural complexity.

## 3. Scope

- The work covered iOS, Android, desktop web, and mobile web.
- The app story should be treated as one shared stream with different proof points: iOS established the clearest baseline win first, while Android later showed a narrower +12% new-user lead signal in Germany rather than one clean app-wide rollout story.
- The web story should be treated separately where impact is discussed: web launched later as a narrower baseline release, with richer enhancements explicitly deferred.
- This was a staged cross-platform programme, not one simultaneous launch.

## 4. My role

- The safest claim from the current source set is that Eddie was a named design stakeholder in the cross-surface work and was involved in the direction-setting context around it.
- The work itself required senior product design judgment across product, engineering, research, and analytics: defining the baseline, shaping platform differences, and helping teams align around rollout decisions.
- Stronger first-person claims about end-to-end ownership, authorship, or sole decision authority should be paired with separate role evidence outside this source set.

## 5. What changed

- The team stopped treating this as a simple UI refresh and reframed it as a baseline reset for a core search system.
- On iOS, a weaker earlier direction was replaced with a clearer filtering interaction that became the basis for rollout.
- On Android, the product did not blindly mirror iOS. The interaction model was adapted to Android-specific behavior and supporting evidence.
- On web, the team shipped a baseline first and deliberately left richer enhancements for later.
- Across the programme, the work aimed to improve clarity for buyers while also simplifying the system and making future experimentation faster.

## 5A. Important workstream: make/model taxonomy

- Within the broader New Search reset, an important subproblem was the make-and-model experience under a richer vehicle taxonomy.
- The older make-to-model interaction stopped scaling once buyers needed to express deeper distinctions such as generation, variant, and engine choice.
- This should not be framed as a backend taxonomy exercise or as "adding more filters." The design problem was helping buyers express vehicle intent more precisely without getting lost.
- The strongest framing is senior-level systems thinking across information architecture, interaction model, search and drilldown behavior, grouped dimensions, visible selection management, multi-select behavior, and rollout complexity.
- Do not overstate the outcome. The work is still ongoing, the experiment picture is mixed, and it is better presented as a strategically important workstream inside New Search than as a standalone resolved win.

## 6. Strongest outcome signals

- iOS is the clearest proof point. By late August 2024, the benchmark had selected a winning direction, and internal materials consistently framed the result as about +6% lead improvement from the list page. A later launch announcement states that the new iOS search and filters experience was fully rolled out across AutoScout24 markets and white labels.
- The same iOS benchmark reported flat total detail-page views, so the result should be framed as a stronger lead signal rather than uniform uplift across every KPI.
- Android evidence is narrower but still useful. The clearest external highlight is a +12% new-user lead signal in Germany, while later benchmark summaries say leads stayed stable in Germany and Italy overall and the manual-save variant performed more weakly. Research favored automatic saving, but the measurable signal is the stronger portfolio headline.
- Web launch communications state that the new baseline went live in Germany and Italy in February 2026 and summarize a recent Italy benchmark as +7% total dealer leads from the list page on desktop, with mobile web leads and detail-page views stable.
- Separate strategy material positioned New Search as formal growth work with modeled upside. Those figures are useful business-case context, not delivered outcomes.

## 7. Platform nuance

- iOS is the strongest and cleanest story: benchmark win, measurable signal, and direct rollout evidence.
- Android is more of a narrower impact story than a shipment story in the current corpus. The clearest supportable highlight is the +12% new-user lead signal in Germany, but the evidence still does not support a clean post-launch Android impact narrative.
- Web is a phased convergence story. The baseline shipped, the clearest reported gain is on desktop, and the release was explicitly positioned as incomplete by design.
- Later writing should not flatten app and web into one identical solution or one identical result.

## 8. Important caveats

- Use `about +6% leads` as the headline iOS framing rather than a range, but keep the qualification. The rounded headline is not perfectly reconciled across sources, so it should still be treated as rounded internal summary framing rather than a fully reconciled exact figure.
- Do not claim a corpus-proven Android launch date or post-launch business impact. The current evidence is strongest at research, benchmark-summary, and rollout-recommendation level.
- Do not present the web +7% result as a full experiment readout. It comes from launch communication summaries rather than the deeper analysis itself.
- Web rollout evidence is phased and partial at first. Some later rollout notes still contain planned next steps rather than confirmed completion.
- Do not reuse early background comparisons as verified baseline metrics unless the primary analytics source is available.

## 9. Writing constraints

- Avoid internal labels, file mechanics, source IDs, rollout bookkeeping, and country-by-country reporting unless they directly strengthen credibility.
- Avoid framing the work as one neat cross-platform launch or as one uniform performance win.
- Emphasize the strategic reset: a fragmented search system became a stronger baseline for decision quality, lead generation, simplification, and faster iteration.
- If the make/model taxonomy work appears in the case study, frame it as a strategically important workstream within the reset rather than a separate flagship case.
- Emphasize the judgment calls: reset after weak evidence, align where it mattered, diverge where platform behavior justified it, and ship the web baseline before the fuller vision.
- Use first-person ownership carefully. Keep direct leadership claims specific, and use team language for rollout and shared outcomes unless stronger role evidence is added.
- Lead with stakes, major decisions, and strongest results. Compress research steps, internal process detail, and timeline mechanics aggressively.
