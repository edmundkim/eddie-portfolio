# Timeline

## How to read this timeline

This is the date-led view of the project between `overview.md` and the fuller evidence in `facts.md`. Exact dates are used only where directly supported, approximate or inferred dates stay approximate, and planned items are labelled as such.

## 2022-2023 background

- **Q4 2022 to Q3 2023 · iOS / Android · Background** Earlier app search-mask work produced mixed signals. Android showed at least one positive result, while several iOS iterations hurt top-line lead metrics even as saved searches improved. This is the background to the later New Search reset.
- **05 Dec 2023 to 25 Dec 2023 · iOS · Benchmark** A live old-vs-new no-toggle test ran for new users across all countries. Later materials position it as the unsuccessful pre-reset benchmark that the new-baseline work needed to replace.

## 2024 reset and iOS validation

- **Jan 2024 · Cross-platform · Research** An early search-mask brief framed the problem across desktop, mWeb, iOS, and Android, and planned to use recording review to prioritise Q1 and Q2 experiments.
- **Jun to Aug 2024 · iOS · Benchmark** A new iOS no-toggle benchmark compared the old control against two redesigned variants, with Variation 2 centred on Done/Finish rather than Delete/Clear. The test belongs in this period, though its exact run length is not fully reconciled.
- **28 Aug 2024 · iOS · Result** The benchmark readout selected Variation 2 as the winner over both the old control and Variation 1. Internal summaries consistently frame the result as a meaningful enquiry lift from List Page, though the exact rounded headline varies slightly by source.
- **Late Aug to early Sep 2024 · iOS · Planning** A stakeholder summary translated the winning benchmark into rollout intent, with all markets targeted first and white labels immediately after. This was still a rollout plan, not shipped-state confirmation.
- **02 Oct 2024 · iOS · Rollout** An internal launch announcement says the new iOS search and filters experience had been fully rolled out to all AutoScout24 markets and white labels. The same communication frames the change as both a KPI win and a codebase simplification that would speed up future experimentation.
- **Oct 2024, approximate · Android · Planning** A planning deck scoped Android benchmarking across DE, IT, NL, BE, and AT for new and existing users, with broader rollout conditional on successful results.
- **Oct 2024, approximate · Desktop / mWeb · Planning** A parallel web planning snapshot says the first desktop designs did not pass UXR, mWeb designs had already been validated, the first phase excluded Make/Model/Variant, and development plus benchmarking were targeted for November and January.

## 2025 Android expansion and web build-up

- **2025, exact month not secure · Cross-platform · Strategy** `New baseline search` appears as a named initiative under `Efficient search execution`, tied to a +10% dealer-lead objective and modelled as a material growth opportunity. This is roadmap framing rather than delivery proof.
- **12 May 2025 to 19 May 2025 · Android · Research** A Germany-based usability study compared AutoSave and non-AutoSave beta variants. Both were generally well received, but AutoSave created less friction and fewer user errors.
- **May 2025 · Android · Decision** The Android UXR recommendation was to move forward with AutoSave, while adding clearer onboarding or highlighting because users often did not notice that filters were being saved automatically.
- **May 2025 · Desktop / mWeb · Planning** A web status update says foundational work was nearly complete, the first phase was expected to finish in July 2025, and the first old-vs-new comparison was targeted for mid-August with initial results expected in September.
- **Jul to Aug 2025 · Android · Benchmark** Later Android benchmark material compared AutoSave and no-AutoSave in Germany and Italy. The safest reading is that AutoSave kept dealer and private leads stable, produced a +12% new-user enquiry signal in Germany, and outperformed no-AutoSave there.
- **Jul to Aug 2025 · Android · Planning** A rollout update recommended proceeding with AutoSave and framed rollout to core markets by 08 Aug as the next step. The visible corpus supports recommendation and rollout intent, not a confirmed launch.
- **2025, exact date uncertain · Android · Result detail** An Italy analytics workbook shows one Android variation with no statistically significant enquiry uplift and several statistically significant declines in browsing and list-page interaction metrics. Because the workbook does not map that variation cleanly to a named UX pattern, it sharpens caution more than chronology.

## 2026 web rollout and follow-through

- **04 Feb 2026 · Desktop / mWeb · Result** A dated web status update said the initial Italy benchmark signal showed the new search performing at least as well as the old search, with a fuller deep-dive promised by 09 Feb.
- **05 Feb 2026 · Desktop / mWeb · Rollout** A dated launch email says New Search went live in Germany and Italy on desktop and mWeb, cars only. The same communication bundle summarises the Italy benchmark as about +7% desktop dealer leads from List Page, with mWeb leads and DPVs stable.
- **05 Feb 2026 · Desktop / mWeb · Launch framing** The web release was explicitly positioned as a 2026 baseline rather than a finished end state, with sticky filters, filter visualisations, recommendations, and related improvements planned later.
- **Mar 2026 · Desktop / mWeb · Rollout** A later launch note says New Search was then launched for Belgium, the Netherlands, and Austria, again for cars only.
- **Mar 2026 · Desktop / mWeb · Follow-up** That same note targeted rollout to remaining markets and non-car List Pages by the end of the following week. In the visible corpus this remains planned next-step language, not confirmed completion.

## Remaining uncertainties

- The 2024 iOS benchmark clearly produced a win, but the reusable headline uplift varies slightly across benchmark, strategy, and launch materials.
- The exact runtime of the 2024 iOS benchmark is not fully reconciled, even though its place in the Jun to Aug 2024 window is clear.
- Android evidence reaches UXR, benchmarking, recommendation, and rollout planning, but the visible corpus does not include a clean indexed Android launch confirmation.
- The later 2026 web expansion note is clearly after the 05 Feb Germany and Italy launch, but its exact in-body send date is not visible.
