# Notes

## Initial judgment
- Category: supporting
- Confidence: medium
- One-line verdict: Credible zero-to-one service-design work with senior stakeholder exposure, but it stays too conceptual and too lightly evidenced to be more than supporting legacy material.

## What this project proves
- You can turn an unclear new business model into a structured acquisition and lifecycle experience across desktop and mobile web.
- You can combine research, competitive review, flows, wireframes, and user testing quickly in a two-month greenfield engagement.
- You can lead UX definition with senior client stakeholders while coordinating with strategy, architecture, and visual design.

## What weakens it
- It is still pre-MVP concept work; the main outcomes are client approval and helping win follow-on work, not user or business impact in market.
- The written case leans on generic process language, and many of the strongest artifacts appear trapped in images rather than in the text.
- Validation is fairly light: seven participants, broad positive quotes, and a 4.5/5 rating are not especially strong proof for a senior portfolio.

## Overlap with current portfolio
- It adds a zero-to-one mobility-subscription and service-lifecycle story that your current AutoScout case studies do not show as directly.
- It still overlaps with stronger newer work on systems thinking, ambiguous problem framing, and cross-functional leadership, but with weaker outcome evidence.

## Recommended placement
- Keep it only as compact supporting legacy work if you want one consulting-era greenfield mobility concept in the mix.
- Best fit is Earlier Work, not Selected Works; if the legacy section stays very tight, Archive is also defensible.
- It is worth further rewriting only if you can make the core service-design decisions and what survived into the MVP much clearer.

## Rewrite direction
- Reframe it around the specific UX problem of making a novel subscription model understandable and lower-friction from selection through lifecycle management.
- Compress the "future of mobility," double-diamond, and generic process narration; cut anything that does not show a decision, constraint, or tradeoff.
- Lead with the ambiguous business model, the end-to-end funnel/lifecycle scope, the executive-facing concept alignment, and the few concrete testing findings.

## Open questions
- How much of this concept actually carried into the MVP after Deloitte won the follow-on work?
- Can you surface clearer evidence of which structural decisions were yours versus shared with the strategist, UI designer, and leadership?
