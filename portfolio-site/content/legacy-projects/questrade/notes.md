# Notes

## Initial judgment
- Category: archive
- Confidence: medium
- One-line verdict: Strong evidence of large-scale content and IA rigor, but too little proof of shipped design impact to earn a prominent place in a senior portfolio.

## What this project proves
- You can audit and restructure a large, messy web ecosystem with real scale.
- You can work across strategy, content, design, and client teams in a complex financial domain.
- You can create operational artifacts that help sprint teams plan, align, and execute.

## What weakens it
- The outcome is thin: the main result is that a document was approved and used, not that a user or business metric improved.
- The work shown is heavily process and inventory focused, with limited evidence of product design judgment or final experience quality.
- Confidentiality limits and unrecoverable images make it hard to assess how strong the actual deliverables were.

## Overlap with current portfolio
- It adds a useful signal around information architecture, content systems, and handling complexity at enterprise website scale.
- It will feel redundant if newer projects already show systems thinking, stakeholder leadership, and transformation work with clearer shipped outcomes.

## Recommended placement
- Keep it only if you want one older example that proves content architecture and operational rigor.
- It belongs in Archive or Earlier Work, not Selected Works.
- It is only worth further rewriting if you specifically need a content strategy / IA case in the mix.

## Rewrite direction
- Reframe it around simplifying a complex financial content ecosystem, not around generic digital transformation language.
- Compress the long discovery and team-process setup; cut vague strategy framing that does not prove much on its own.
- Lead with the concrete scale of the audit, the mapping artifact, and the specific decisions or handoff value it enabled.

## Open questions
- What specific changes to the site structure, content model, or backlog came directly from this work?
- Are there clearer artifacts or screenshots that show the quality of the mapping and IA decisions without breaking confidentiality?
