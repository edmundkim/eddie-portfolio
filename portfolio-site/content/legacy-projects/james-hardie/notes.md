# Notes

## Initial judgment
- Category: supporting
- Confidence: medium
- One-line verdict: This has real enterprise and systems-thinking signal, but the case is too broad, too process-heavy, and too light on outcomes to be stronger than supporting work.

## What this project proves
- You can work on a messy B2B2C ecosystem with multiple audiences, legacy constraints, and cross-channel journeys.
- You can shape both customer-facing and operational experiences, not just a marketing site.
- You can contribute to governance foundations such as a digital style guide and shared product information structure.

## What weakens it
- The narrative is diffuse and tries to cover too many workstreams at once, so your actual contribution does not land cleanly.
- There are no concrete shipped outcomes, adoption numbers, or business results to prove the value of the work.
- The case is presented as an ongoing, confidential reinterpretation, which weakens credibility and makes the evidence feel indirect.

## Overlap with current portfolio
- It may add useful signal around enterprise transformation, B2B ordering flows, and systems-level thinking across consumer and internal touchpoints.
- It will feel redundant if your newer work already shows strategy, cross-functional leadership, and design systems with clearer outcomes.

## Recommended placement
- Keep it only as supporting legacy work if you want one example of complex enterprise service and platform design.
- It belongs in Earlier Work or a tightly curated Archive, not in Selected Works.
- It is worth further rewriting only if you can make the ordering portal, B2B2C model, and governance angle much sharper.

## Rewrite direction
- Reframe it around one core story: designing a coherent digital system across consumer discovery, retail ordering, and governance.
- Cut a lot of the generic consulting-process narration and compress the long discovery and methodology sections.
- Lead with the B2B2C complexity, the ordering portal constraints, the product-information problem, and the design-system/governance contribution.

## Open questions
- What actually shipped, and were any operational or customer outcomes measured after launch?
- Which decisions or artifacts were primarily yours versus shared with the other designer, partner firm, and broader delivery team?
