## What I like

- The circular `VIEW` CTA creates a very clear interaction target over the project preview.
- The hover behavior has a slightly bouncy feel that gives the interaction personality.
- The motion feels softer and more playful than a standard fade or scale, which helps the project tile feel more intentional.

## What to study

- How the circle enters, settles, and tracks the user interaction over the project area.
- The relationship between the hover delay and the feeling of polish.
- Whether the CTA movement is driven by scale, easing, slight lag, cursor-follow behavior, or a mix of those.

## Motion / interaction notes

- The interaction reads as slightly delayed, which adds softness but may be a bit too slow.
- The small bounce is the key part worth borrowing; it gives the CTA a tactile feel.
- The circle overlay is large enough to feel like a deliberate moment, not just a small affordance.
- If adapted, the motion should likely feel a little more responsive than the reference while keeping the same character.

## What to avoid copying

- Do not copy the exact circle size, timing curve, or hover choreography one-to-one.
- Avoid making the delay so long that the interface feels sluggish.
- Do not add this interaction everywhere; it should be reserved for moments where a project preview needs a stronger invitation to click.

## Relevance to my portfolio

- This could pair well with the David Pacheco-inspired layout direction by giving each case-study entry point a more distinctive hover moment.
- It is especially relevant if featured projects become large, scroll-led entry sections instead of small cards.
- The main lesson is not "use a circle," but "make the CTA feel physical, deliberate, and slightly playful."

## Notes for adaptation

- Test a faster version of this interaction with less delay.
- Keep the motion restrained enough that it still fits a minimal, high-quality portfolio.
- Consider whether the CTA should say `VIEW`, `OPEN`, or simply rely on the project title plus motion cue.
- If the project tiles already have strong scroll behavior, this hover interaction should complement that system rather than compete with it.
