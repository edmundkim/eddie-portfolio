## What I like

- Strong headline hierarchy with generous spacing and a calm reading rhythm.
- Motion feels polished, but the page still scans quickly.
- The overall feel is premium without becoming loud or overly decorative.

## What to study

- How the page moves from a simple hero into denser proof sections without feeling abrupt.
- How typography, spacing, and motion work together to create perceived quality.
- How supporting visuals stay subordinate to the core message.

## Motion / interaction notes

- Scroll reveals appear restrained and staged rather than flashy.
- Hover states feel fast and crisp, which makes the interface feel more intentional.
- Motion seems to support hierarchy and pacing, not just decoration.

## What to avoid copying

- Do not borrow the exact dark product-marketing aesthetic.
- Do not mimic the brand voice or use similar gradients just because they look polished.
- Avoid adding motion everywhere; the lesson here is restraint.

## Relevance to my portfolio

- Helpful for shaping a more editorial and confident first impression.
- Especially relevant for hero pacing and for how selected work could be introduced with more clarity and presence.
